About

I am a mechanical engineer and Engineering MBA candidate with experience spanning construction, upstream energy, and operational analytics. My background has been shaped by environments where engineering decisions directly impact safety, cost, and long-term asset performance.

I earned my Bachelor of Science in Mechanical Engineering from Texas A&M University and passed the Fundamentals of Engineering (FE) exam, building a strong foundation in technical analysis, systems thinking, and problem solving. During my undergraduate education and early professional experiences, I became increasingly interested in roles that bridge engineering execution with business and operational decision-making.

Professionally, I have worked in both heavy civil construction and upstream oil and gas. At Kiewit, I served as a Pipe Field/Office Engineer Intern on a large municipal water infrastructure project. My responsibilities included pipe procurement forecasting, surveying for installations, pretest walkdowns, and coordination of daily activities for a large craft workforce.

More recently, I completed an internship with Formentera Operations in the upstream energy sector. There, I focused on operational analytics by designing a well failure tracking system and developing a live critical gas rate calculator to support gas lift optimization. This experience reinforced my interest in using data and engineering principles to improve reliability and decision quality at scale.

I am currently pursuing the Anthony Bahr ’91 Engineering MBA at Mays Business School to further develop my skills in finance, operations, and organizational leadership. My goal is to build a career at the intersection of engineering, analytics, and business, contributing to operational excellence and long-term value creation.
